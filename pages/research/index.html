RESEARCH FRONTIERS

Advanced Materials for Energy Storage and Sensors

At the Device Fabrication and Characterization Lab (DFCL), our core research mission is centered on designing, microfabricating, and evaluating advanced functional materials for high-performance energy storage devices (supercapacitors, Li-ion, Na-ion, solid-state batteries) and smart chemical/gas sensor platforms.

Solid-State Energy Storage

Synthesis of 2D MXenes, MOF derivatives, and bio-char composite electrodes for high-energy-density supercapacitors and hybrid batteries.

Smart Gas & Chemical Sensors

Chemiresistive and optical sensors engineered for sub-ppm H₂ gas leak detection, environmental monitoring, and biosensors.

Device Fabrication & Micro-Rigs

In-house thin-film deposition, spin-coating, screen printing, coin-cell assembly, and in situ impedance spectroscopy.
