Advanced Materials for Energy Storage and Sensors

At the Device Fabrication and Characterization Lab (DFCL), MIT Manipal, we synthesize functional nanomaterials, fabricate solid-state supercapacitors & battery electrodes, and engineer ultra-sensitive gas & biosensors.

CORE DOMAINS

Research Frontiers

Pioneering material architectures for high-density energy storage devices, micro-supercapacitors, and IoT sensor integration.

Primary Focus

Energy Storage Materials & Solid-State Batteries

Synthesis of MXenes, 2D MOFs, and bio-char composites for hybrid supercapacitors, metal-sulfur batteries, and fast-charging solid-state electrolytes.

View Energy Storage Research arrow_forward

Smart Gas & Electrochemical Sensors

Chemiresistive and optical sensors for sub-ppm H₂ leak detection and biomarker sensing.

Microfabrication & Probe Rigs

Sputtering, CVD, spin-coating, and in situ impedance spectroscopy characterization.
